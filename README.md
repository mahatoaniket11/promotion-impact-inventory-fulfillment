# Promotion Impact on Inventory & Fulfillment

## Project Impact

This project analyzes how promotional campaigns influence demand volatility, inventory depletion, and fulfillment performance within a retail supply chain. Using a synthetic retail dataset and an end‑to‑end analytics workflow, the analysis quantifies how marketing campaigns affect operational stability across inventory planning and warehouse fulfillment.

The goal is to translate promotion-driven demand patterns into operational insights that help organizations coordinate marketing initiatives with inventory planning and fulfillment capacity.

---

## Business Problem

Retail promotions are a powerful revenue lever, but they also introduce operational risk within supply chains. Promotional campaigns can create sudden demand spikes that exceed available inventory or warehouse fulfillment capacity. When this happens, companies experience stockouts, delayed fulfillment, and lost sales.

These disruptions often occur because marketing campaigns and supply chain planning operate on different timelines. While promotions are designed to increase demand, inventory systems may not be prepared to absorb sudden demand volatility.

This project evaluates how promotional campaigns influence demand behavior, inventory health, and fulfillment performance, and provides insights that support better coordination between marketing and supply chain planning.

## Key Business Questions

* How much does demand increase during promotional campaigns?
* Do promotions significantly increase demand volatility?
* Are promoted products more likely to experience stockouts?
* How does fulfillment performance change during promotion periods?
* What operational strategies can reduce disruption during promotions?

## Analytical Approach

1. Construct a retail dataset simulating orders, promotions, and inventory activity
2. Clean and transform raw datasets using SQL
3. Analyze demand patterns before, during, and after promotion windows
4. Evaluate inventory depletion and stockout events
5. Assess fulfillment performance during promotional demand spikes
6. Deliver insights through an interactive Power BI dashboard

---

## Key Metrics

    -  Demand Lift = (Promotion Demand − Baseline Demand) / Baseline Demand

    - Stockout Risk = Stockout Events / Total Promotion Days

    -  Fulfillment Delay Rate = Delayed Shipments / Total Shipments

    - Demand Volatility = Standard deviation of daily order volume per product

## Key Insights

* Promotions increased average order volume by **~38%** relative to baseline demand levels.
* Demand volatility (standard deviation of daily orders) increased by **approximately 2.4x** during promotion windows.
* Products with discounts greater than **25% experienced stockout rates nearly 3x higher** than non‑promoted products.
* Inventory depletion accelerated significantly during multi‑day promotion campaigns, creating short‑term warehouse imbalances.

---

## Business Impact

The analysis highlights operational risks created by promotion-driven demand spikes and provides insights that help marketing and supply chain teams coordinate promotion planning with inventory availability.

Organizations can use these findings to:

* Introduce **promotion eligibility thresholds based on inventory coverage**
* Incorporate **promotion uplift factors into demand forecasting models**
* Adjust **safety stock levels for promotion‑sensitive SKUs**
* Monitor **inventory and fulfillment risk during large campaign windows**

These changes help organizations capture promotional revenue while minimizing operational disruptions.
